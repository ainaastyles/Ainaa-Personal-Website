AESPA DISCOGRAPHY

FAVOURITE SONGS

List of my favourite aespa songs.

1. Lucid Dream

"Lucid Dream" by aespa is a song about being lost in a dream-like state and always yearning for something or someone. The lyrics depict a sense of confusion and longing, as the members of aespa express their sentiments of isolation and uncertainty. This song speaks to me because it passionately conveys the sense of desire and the complexities of emotions. The dreamy and ethereal quality of the music, mixed with the powerful vocals of the aespa members, makes for a mesmerising and immersive listening experience.

2. Thirsty

aespa's "Thirsty" explores the theme of yearning and desire for someone. The lyrics explain the members of aespa's desire for a person, as reflected by their thirst. They use ocean and wave metaphors to convey their fascination and curiosity for this individual. Winter opens the song by expressing her desire to reach out to this person, recognising the beauty and mystery in their gaze. She likens herself to a violent wind that upsets their feelings. Winter knows that this person radiates throughout the day, heightening their appeal.

3. I'm Unhappy

"I'm Unhappy" by aespa delves at the feelings of dissatisfaction and sadness that can occur from comparing oneself to others on social media. The lyrics depict the narrator's sense of monotony and boredom, with the line "My timeline has come to a stop, day and night drag on" implying a lack of thrill or fulfilment. The song discusses the hardships and dissatisfaction that many of us encounter on a daily basis, as well as how we may overcome them. It is a reminder to be loyal to oneself and not darken one's light for others.

4. Welcome To My World

"Welcome to My World" by aespa is a song about unification, crossing boundaries, and accepting differences. The lyrics describe a fascinating and enticing universe into which the artists invite the listener. This song speaks to me because it eloquently conveys the sense of desire and the complexities of emotions. The dreamy and ethereal quality of the music, mixed with the powerful vocals of the aespa members, makes for a mesmerising and immersive listening experience.

5. Spicy

"Spicy" by aespa embodies the essence of empowerment and self-assurance, setting boundaries and refusing to take anything less than what one deserves. The lyrics portray aespa members as strong, confident, and unapologetic people. They exude a sense of empowerment and control, refusing to allow others determine their worth or manipulate their emotions. The repeated reference to being "too spicy" indicates their extroverted and assertive demeanour, which can be daunting for others who are not prepared to deal with it. Through catchy choruses and strong verses, "Spicy" becomes an anthem for embracing one's identity, defining boundaries, and standing up for oneself in the face of insecurity or doubt.

6. Drama

aespa's "Drama" delves into themes of empowerment, self-expression, and breaking free from societal expectations and limits. The lyrics show confidence and resolve as the members of aespa proclaim their individuality and embrace their distinct personalities. The initial sentence, "I'm the drama," implies that aespa is ready to take centre stage and leave their mark. This song empowers and inspires me. It encourages listeners to accept their own "drama" and conquer any difficulties or traumas in their lives to live genuinely and freely.

AESPA ALBUM REVIEW

aespa "Armageddon" 1st Full Album Review

"Armageddon" was eagerly anticipated, given the length of time it took to arrive and the typically high quality that aespa has gave us. aespa shines most when they embrace their most edgy, striking sounds. Tracks like "Supernova" combine high fashion and a grungy mood, with revving sound effects and poised synths. The title track, "Armageddon," maintains its low-slung, high-attitude style while smoothly moving between softer periods. Furthermore, "Set The Tone" sounds like the ideal beginning music for a massive brawl, with lines like "We set the tone / Music on where we go / Crazy beat drum." The album is a story of progress and empowerment. In "Mine," aespa focuses on overcoming fear and insecurity to eventually stand strong. "Prologue" encourages listeners to follow their own path rather than comparing themselves to others—an important lesson in today's social media-driven world. Similarly, "Live My Life" emphasises doing things their own way, with each member serving as the main character in their own life. While some tracks are experimental, others venture into more mainstream terrain. "Bahama" is a breezy, bright summer pop song about bringing a buddy on a trip to the named island. The catchy chorus—"Take me, take me / Take me on a / Ocean blue / Bahama, ba ba / Bahama, hama"—makes it an immediate hit for any summer playlist. Overall, "Armageddon" meets expectations, demonstrating aespa's development, confidence, and versatility. As a fan, I am excited to see how they evolve and explore with their sound!

aespa "Drama" 4th Mini Album Review

On November 10, 2023, aespa released their fourth mini album, 'Drama'. "Drama" has a captivating, electric energy. It is not like the previous title tracks by Aespa. Even though it still adheres to the classic slogan of female emancipation, its presentation is more engaging. The record quickly shifts gears to "Trick or Trick," a punch line. A Halloween-themed song with fun lyrics and a powerful bassline. Tackling the hyperpop genre, "Trick or Trick" is a fun song to listen to. "You," a romantic, soft song with a shimmering vocal performance and a laid-back guitar lead, serves as the album's finale. Giselle, Winter, Ningning, and Karina all feel regal as they sing in appreciation to their fans. 'Drama - The Fourth Mini Album' is a wonderful addition to the aespa universe. It showcases the four girls' vocal prowess and musical versatility. Although the plot became little convoluted, the overall energy, particularly from the title track 'Drama', was uplifting and left the listener wanting more.

aespa "MY WORLD" 3rd Mini Album Review

"MY WORLD" is aespa's third mini-album, released in 2023. This album marks a significant step in aespa's musical journey, showcasing their ability to deliver big songs with infectious hooks. The album includes six songs, each trying to outdo the others, demonstrating aespa's versatility. Standout tracks like "Salty & Sweet" and "Thirsty" show aespa's unique approach to music, blending industrial synth grinds with unconventional instrumental approaches. "I'm Unhappy" captures feelings of melancholy and longing, using the digital world as a metaphor for feeling disconnected. Overall, "MY WORLD" is a testament to aespa's growth as artists and their bid for main pop girl status.

aespa "Girls" 2nd Mini Album Review

"Girls" is aespa's second mini-album, released in 2022. This album includes their debut song "Black Mamba", their remake of Yoo Young-jin's R&B ballad "Forever", and their cover of S.E.S.'s "Dreams Come True". However, the album has been criticized for playing it too safe, lacking the intense grip on your attention as "Next Level" did. Despite this, the album shows off fresh approaches in aespa's music. "Lingo" mixes flashes of country into their futuristic pop, while "Life's Too Short" and "ICU" are pretty but safe guitar-led pop tracks.

aespa "Savage" 1st Mini Album Review

"Savage" is aespa's first mini-album, released in 2021. This album sees the quartet dive head-first into electrifying hyperpop and cutting-edge production. It's a bold and innovative album that stakes aespa's claim for greatness with its future-facing cyberpunk theme.
